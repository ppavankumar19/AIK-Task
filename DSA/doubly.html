Discover the Six Tastes of Ayurveda: The Shadrasa Framework

Did you know that the taste of your food does more than just make your tongue happy? In Ayurveda, an ancient Indian system of medicine, there's a unique idea called the adrasa Framework (pronounced Shah-dra-sah). This framework teaches that there are six main tastes, and understanding them is key to keeping your body and mind healthy and balanced!


What is Rasa?

The word Rasa means 'to relish or taste,' and it also means 'juice, sap, or secretion'. When something first touches your tongue, the initial flavor you experience is called rasa.

An important Ayurvedic sage, Maharishi Punarvasu treya, was key in bringing these six different tastes together into the six adrasas. These six tastes affect both your physical and mental well-being, and they even have an emotional connection.


The Six Shadrasas (Tastes)

ras svdvamlalavaatiktoaakayaka dravyamritste ca yathprva balvah


The six tastes (a-ras) are sweet (svdu), sour (amla), salty (lavaa), bitter (tikta), pungent/spicy (ua), and astringent (kaya). These tastes arise from the combinations of the five elements and are properties of substances (dravya-rit).


The phrase "yath-prvam balvah" means that each taste nourishes and strengthens the body in the order listed, with sweet providing the greatest strength and each subsequent taste giving relatively less strength.



Ayurveda identifies six primary tastes:

  1. Madhura (Sweet)
  2. What it is: The sweet taste is pleasing to everyone and comes from sugars and carbohydrates.
  3. Body/Mind Effects: It gives you strength and energy, and can bring feelings of love and compassion (in moderation). Too much can lead to laziness or possessiveness.
  4. Found in: Ripe bananas, milk, rice, and honey.
  5. Amla (Sour)
  6. What it is: The taste that makes your face pucker! It comes from acids in food.
  7. Body/Mind Effects: In moderation, it warms the body and helps your digestion and liver work well. Too much can cause thirst and jealousy.
  8. Found in: Raw mango, lemon, tomatoes, and curd (yogurt).
  9. Lavaa (Salty)
  10. What it is: The salty taste is important for making food taste good and for balancing electrolytes.
  11. Body/Mind Effects: It strengthens muscles and supports digestion. It can bring courage and enthusiasm. Eating too much causes water retention.
  12. Found in: Rock salt, sea salt, and seaweed.
  13. Kau (Pungent)
  14. What it is: This is the hot, spicy taste that comes from volatile oils (like in peppers).
  15. Body/Mind Effects: It clears sinuses, promotes sweating, and supports digestion. It can bring vigor. Too much can cause anger or stomach ulcers.
  16. Found in: Chillies, ginger, pepper, and garlic.
  17. Tikta (Bitter)
  18. What it is: The bitter tastenot always a favorite, especially for kids! It tends to be cooling.
  19. Body/Mind Effects: It cleanses your body, tones muscles, and brings clarity and self-awareness. Too much can lead to giddiness or depression.
  20. Found in: Bitter gourd and fenugreek seeds.
  21. Kaya (Astringent)
  22. What it is: This is the taste that gives you a dry, chalky feeling in your mouth. It's found in foods rich in tannins.
  23. Body/Mind Effects: It helps absorb excess moisture, clots blood, and brings stability. Too much can cause nervousness or muscle discomfort.
  24. Found in: Pomegranate, unripe bananas, and cinnamon.
