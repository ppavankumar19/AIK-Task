Understanding Tridoa

Ayurveda explains that three special forces called doa govern the working of the human body and mind.


The word doa comes from the idea duayanti iti dothat which can get disturbed and then disturb the body.


A simple way to understand this is to think of homeostasis, the bodys natural ability to keep everything in balancetemperature, digestion, sleep, and even emotions. When homeostasis is steady, we are healthy. When it is disturbed, we fall sick.


Another example is a tripod stand: if all three legs are strong and equal, it stands firmly; if even one leg becomes weak, the whole stand becomes unstable. The three doas behave just like this tripod.

Ayurveda describes the Tridoa in a verse:


: :

vyu: pitta kaphaceti trayo do: samsata|

viktvikt deha ghnanti te varttayanti ca ||


This means: Vyu (Vta), Pitta, and Kapha are the three doas. When they are balanced, they protect the body; when they become disturbed, they cause diseases.


So, balance brings health, and imbalance brings illness.

The Three Doas

Vyu Vta: Vta is made of ka (space) and Vyu (air). It is responsible for all movementbreathing, blinking, blood flow, talking, walking, and even the movement of thoughts. Vta keeps the body active and creative.


Pitta: Pitta is made of Agni (fire) and a little Jala (water). It controls digestion, body temperature, intelligence, and transformation. Whenever something is heated or processed in the body, Pitta is at work.


Kapha: Kapha comes from Jala (water) and Pthv (earth). It gives the body strength, stability, growth, smoothness, and calmness. Bones, muscles, lubrication, and emotional steadiness all depend on Kapha.


Together, Vta, Pitta, and Kapha form the three legs of health. Understanding them helps us know how to keep our body balanced, energetic, and healthy throughout life.
