Understanding the Qualities of the Three Doas

Ayurveda describes three doasVta, Pitta, and Kaphaeach with its own qualities. These qualities help us understand how the body behaves and why we feel the way we do.

======================================================================

1. Vta Doa Movement & Lightness

Qualities (Guas):

Light, dry, cold, fast, rough, subtle, mobile.

When Vta Increases (Too Much Vta):

  1. Feeling anxious, jumpy, restless
  2. Dry skin, dry lips, dry hair
  3. Gas, bloating, constipation
  4. Sleeplessness or light sleep
  5. Difficulty focusing
  6. Example: A student skipping meals and sleeping late may feel nervous or unable to sit stillclassic high Vta.


When Vta Decreases (Too Little Vta):

  1. Slow movement, sluggish behaviour
  2. Feeling dull or low energy
  3. Poor circulation
  4. Difficulty getting started with activities
  5. Example: A very inactive day with no movement may make the body feel stiff or slow.



2. Pitta Doa Heat & Transformation

Qualities:

Hot, sharp, intense, light, oily, spreading.

When Pitta Increases (Too Much Pitta):

  1. Anger, irritation, impatience
  2. Feeling too hot, sweating easily
  3. Acidity or burning digestion
  4. Redness in skin or eyes
  5. Perfectionist or overly demanding behaviour
  6. Example: Studying in hot weather without enough water can make someone cranky and overheated.


When Pitta Decreases (Too Little Pitta):

  1. Weak digestion (food feels heavy)
  2. Feeling cold
  3. Low motivation
  4. Slow thinking or difficulty concentrating
  5. Example: Eating too many cold foods may reduce digestive heat.


3. Kapha Doa Stability & Strength

Qualities:

Heavy, slow, cool, smooth, steady, oily, soft, stable.

When Kapha Increases (Too Much Kapha):

  1. Feeling sleepy or lazy
  2. Weight gain or swelling
  3. Slow digestion
  4. Excess mucus, cough, or allergies
  5. Over-attachment or difficulty letting go
  6. Example: Eating too many sweets can make someone feel heavy and dull.


When Kapha Decreases (Too Little Kapha):

  1. Feeling weak or unstable
  2. Dryness in joints
  3. Difficulty staying calm
  4. Lack of endurance
  5. Example: Too much exercise without rest may reduce Kaphas stabilizing strength.


======================================================================

Summary

  1. Vta = movement too much = anxiety; too little = sluggishness
  2. Pitta = heat & digestion too much = anger/heat; too little = weak digestion
  3. Kapha = structure & calmness too much = heaviness; too little = weakness


1

IAST: tatra rko laghu ta khara skma calo'nila

Vta is like the windlight, dry, cool, and always moving.



2

IAST: pitta sasneha tkoa laghu visra sara dravam

Pitta is like firehot, sharp, bright, and quick to act.



3

IAST: snigdha to gurur manda lako mtsna sthira kapha

Kapha is like water and earthcool, heavy, smooth, and steady.



4

IAST: sasarga sannipta ca tad-dvitri-kaya-kopata

Illness happens when two or all three doas get disturbed together.
